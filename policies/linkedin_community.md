# LinkedIn Professional Community Policies

## Core Principles

LinkedIn's Professional Community Policies are organized around four core principles:

### 1. Be Safe
- Be civil and respectful in every single interaction
- No hate, discrimination, racism, harassment (including unwanted romantic advances), or bullying
- No threatening or violent behavior
- No promotion of self-harm or suicide
- No child sexual abuse material (CSAM)
- No terrorism content or promotion of terrorist organizations
- No extremely violent content

### 2. Be Trustworthy
- Use your true identity
- Provide accurate information about yourself or your organization
- Only share information that is real and authentic
- No fake profiles or fake accounts
- No fake jobs or fraudulent job postings
- No misinformation or disinformation
- No spam or scams
- No impersonation of others

### 3. Be Professional
- Content must be professionally relevant and on topic
- Appropriate topics include:
  - Sharing and gaining expertise
  - Hiring or how to get hired
  - Learning or teaching new skills
  - Professional discussions and insights
- No explicit, shocking, or inflammatory content
- No sexually explicit or pornographic material
- No gratuitous violence or gore
- Content should maintain professional decorum

### 4. Respect Others' Rights and Follow the Law
- Respect privacy of others
- Respect copyright and intellectual property
- Follow all applicable laws
- No illegal activities
- No unauthorized data collection or scraping
- No unauthorized automation

## Prohibited Content and Behavior

### Harassment and Abuse
- No targeted harassment of individuals
- No coordinated harassment campaigns
- No doxxing (revealing private information)
- No unwanted sexual advances or solicitation
- No stalking behavior

### Hate Speech and Discrimination
- No content attacking people based on:
  - Race or ethnicity
  - National origin
  - Gender or gender identity
  - Sexual orientation
  - Religion
  - Age
  - Disability
  - Veteran status

### Misinformation
- No false claims about public health
- No election misinformation
- No conspiracy theories that could cause real-world harm
- No manipulated media intended to deceive

### AI and Automation Policies (2026 Update)
- AI-assisted content is allowed if it contains original ideas or encourages meaningful conversation
- Generic, repetitive AI-generated content ("AI slop") will be suppressed from recommendations
- Bot-generated comments that add nothing to conversations are prohibited
- Automation tools that generate AI content at scale are restricted
- Content should reflect unique perspectives, context, or expertise
- AI should assist human thought, not replace it entirely

### Spam and Manipulation
- No automated posting without proper disclosure
- No artificial engagement (buying likes, comments, followers)
- No coordinated inauthentic behavior
- No link farms or SEO manipulation schemes

## Enforcement

Violations can result in:
- Content removal
- Visibility restrictions (content not shown in wider feed)
- Content labeling
- Feature restrictions
- Account suspension
- Permanent account ban (for egregious violations)

Repeated violations lead to escalating consequences. Appeals are available for content removal decisions.

## Reporting

Members are encouraged to report violations they encounter in:
- Profiles
- Posts
- Messages
- Comments
- Any other content on the platform
